# early-diabetes-detection-project
One of the most common non-communicable diseases in the world today is diabetes, a chronic metabolic disease. The World Health Organization reports that the prevalence of adult diabetes is gradually increasing worldwide, having a major effect on both public health and economics. Diabetes must be detected early in order to enable prompt therapies that can either avoid or postpone problems such as renal failure, nerve damage, and cardiovascular disorders. Conventional diagnostic techniques, including blood glucose testing, are costly, time-consuming, and intrusive, which restricts their use in environments with limited resources.
In this situation, machine learning models can offer a different method of estimating the risk of developing diabetes. Predictive models that can swiftly and precisely identify high-risk patients can be created by utilizing past health data and sophisticated algorithms. This report's goals are to assess the performance of K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) and Decision Tree classifiers, develop a machine learning model using the Pima Indians Diabetes dataset, and identify the best method for diabetes prediction.
